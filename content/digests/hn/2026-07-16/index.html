Martin Fowler's Unmesh Joshi argues that Domain-Specific Languages are the key to making LLMs reliable, because a constrained syntax dramatically narrows the space of valid outputs and comes with a deterministic validator that lets an agent auto-correct. The HN crowd largely agrees with the premise from experience, with one top thread noting that even a 200-line spec for a brand-new DSL is enough for chatbots to work with it, despite having zero training data on that specific language.
